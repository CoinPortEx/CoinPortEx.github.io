
















  ### Bitcoin Cash ABC (BCHA)
  Bitcoin Cash ABC (BCHA) is a decentralized digital currency, without a central bank or single administrator, that can be sent from user to user on the peer-to-peer bitcoin network without the need for intermediaries.<sup id="cite_ref-JSC_11-1" class="reference">[7]</sup>&nbsp;Transactions are verified by network&nbsp;nodes&nbsp;through&nbsp;cryptography&nbsp;and recorded in a public&nbsp;distributed ledger&nbsp;called a&nbsp;blockchain. The&nbsp;cryptocurrency&nbsp;was invented in 2008 by an unknown person or group of people using the name&nbsp;Satoshi Nakamoto.<sup id="cite_ref-whoissn_13-0" class="reference">[9]</sup>&nbsp;The currency began use in 2009<sup id="cite_ref-NY2011_14-0" class="reference">[10]</sup>&nbsp;when its implementation was released as&nbsp;open-source software.<sup id="cite_ref-Antonopoulos2014_9-1" class="reference">[6]</sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: ch. 1">: ch. 1 </span></sup>
  Bitcoins are created as a reward for a process known as mining. They can be exchanged for other currencies, products, and services,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference">[11]</sup>&nbsp;but the real-world value of the coins is extremely volatile.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_16-0" class="reference">[12]</sup>&nbsp;Research produced by the&nbsp;University of Cambridge&nbsp;estimated that in 2017, there were 2.9 to 5.8 million unique users using a&nbsp;cryptocurrency wallet, most of them using bitcoin.<sup id="cite_ref-CU2017_17-0" class="reference">[13]</sup>
  Bitcoin has been criticized for its use in illegal transactions, the large amount of electricity (and thus&nbsp;carbon footprint) used by mining,&nbsp;price volatility, and thefts from exchanges. Some economists and commentators have characterized it as a&nbsp;speculative bubble&nbsp;at various times. Bitcoin has also been used as an investment, although several regulatory agencies have issued investor alerts about bitcoin.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_16-1" class="reference">[12]</sup><sup id="cite_ref-CFTC_bitcoin_18-0" class="reference">[14]</sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference">[15]</sup>
